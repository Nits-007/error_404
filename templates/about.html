What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases, primarily carbon dioxide, that are emitted directly or indirectly by human activities, and is expressed as an equivalent amount of carbon dioxide (CO2). It's an estimation of the impact our daily activities have on the environment, especially concerning climate change.

Why is it Important?

Greenhouse gases trap heat in the earth's atmosphere, leading to global warming and subsequent climate changes. Human activities, especially burning fossil fuels for electricity, heat, and transportation, are the primary sources of these emissions. By understanding our carbon footprint, we can make informed decisions to reduce it, mitigating the adverse effects of climate change.
